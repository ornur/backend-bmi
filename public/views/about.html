About

BMI is a measure of body fat based on a person's weight and height. The BMI norms for males and females are generally the same. The calculation for BMI is as follows:

BMI = weight(kg) / height(m)2

The World Health Organization (WHO) provides the following BMI categories for both males and females:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 to 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 to 29.9
  • Obesity Class I: BMI 30 to 34.9
  • Obesity Class II: BMI 35 to 39.9
  • Obesity Class III: BMI 40 or greater

While the categories are the same for both genders, it's important to note that BMI has limitations and does not directly measure body fat percentage or distribution. Additionally, the distribution of body fat can vary between males and females, and other factors such as muscle mass and bone density can influence BMI.
